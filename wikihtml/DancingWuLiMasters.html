=======

OK. The term Wu Li is the Chinese word for physics or 'patterns of organic energy'. This term was introduced at an Esalen Institute meeting in 1976 by Tai Chi master Al Huang. Journalist Gary Zukav developed the idea that physics is the dance of the Wu Li masters--the teachers of physical essence. A Wu Li master while 'dancing' with his student does not teach, but, the student learns. He begins at the center, the heart of the matter. The book deals not with knowledge, which is always past tense, but with imagination, which is physics come alive. Most people think that physicists are explaining the universe. Some physicists even think that. However, they are only dancing with it. The book comprises quantum theory, particle physics, and relativity. Zukav: The fact is that physics is not mathematics. Physics, in essence, is simple wonder at the way things are and a driving (some say a compulsive) interest in how that is. Stripped of mathematics, physics becomes pure enchantment.'

The book communicates that enchantment by describing recent developments in advanced physics for those with little or no mathematical or technical knowledge. Zukav draws comparisons between quantum mechanics, modern psychology, and eastern thought.

In a review by Michael Kasor he observes that Zukav occasionally notes the similarity berween science and religion such as in the passage where he explains how sub-atomic particles collide and annihilate each other, and from which new particles are born: "Sub-atomic particles forever partake of this unceasing dance of annihilation and creation. This 20th century discovery, with all its psychedelic implications, is not a new concept. In fact, it is very similar to the way that much of the population, including the Hindus and Buddhists, view their reality."

"Hindu mythology is virtually a large-scale projection into the psychological realm of microscopic scientific discoveries. Deities such as Shiva and Vishnu contually dance the creation and destruction of universes while the Buddhist image of the wheel of life symbolizes the unending process of birth, death, and rebirth which is part of the world of form, which is emptiness, which is form."

A review in Graphic Design notes the astonishingly unscientific perspectives highlighted by Zukav with respect to physics. Note the paragraph, "Newtonian physics and quantum mechanics are partners in a double irony. Newtonian physics is based upon the idea of laws which govern phenomena and the power inherent in understanding them, but it leads to impotence in the face of a Great Machine which is the universe. Quantum mechanics is based upon the ideas of minimal knowledge of future phenomena (we are limited to knowing probabilities) but it leads to the possibility that our reality is what we choose to make it.
