Gustave Eiffel

Alexandre Gustave Eiffel was born in Dijon France in 1832. He graduated from the Ecole Centrale des Arts et Manufactures, Paris in 1855 and then became an engineer for the French western railway company. He established an independent practice in 1864 and built bridges all over the world. He built the Garabit Viaduct of the Truyère River in France from 1880-1884. In 1885 he designed the interior support structure for the Statue of Liberty. He completed the Eiffel tower in 1889.

Eiffel was a master of elegantly constructed wrought-iron lattices, which formed the basis of his bridge constructions and led to his project for the Eiffel Tower. He was mainly recognized as an engineer and bridge builder.

Eiffel died in Paris in 1923.
