Augusto Boal

Rio de Janero Received his PhD from Columbia University. After receiving his doctorate, he bagan work in Sao Paulo Brazile in the Arena Theatre. Boal liked to know the reaction of the audience, and so invited their discussions following the performance. He then began allowing the audience to stop the play and offer suggestions to the actors on how they should perform! He also let them offer up suggestion on changes for the stage and set! He saw theatre as a very interactive process.

1971 Boal wrote The Theatre of the Oppressed. This book offered games and excercises for actors and non-actors alike. After publication, he was arrested, tortured and exiled. He went to Argentina, and then moved on to Europe. In 1981 he organized the first International Festival of the Theatre of the Oppressed in Paris, France. He also began establishing several Centers for the Theatre of the Oppressed.

In 1986 he returned to Rio de Janero, where he established another center for the Theatre of the Opressed and has formed several companies of community based theatrical productions.

Forum Theatre

Forum theatre consists of a presentation of short scenes that represent problems of the particular community. Audience members interact by replacing characters in scenes and by improvising new solutions to the problems being presented.

Image Theatre

Image theatre uses individuals to sculpt events and relationships. Occasionally done to the accompaniment of a narrative.

Legislative Theatre

Legislative theatre is a forum style theatre used to identify key problems in the city. It works very much on the neighborhood level. He used theatre to determine what sorts of legislation was needed to solve the city's problems. These ideas were often then put into legislation.

Invisible Theatre

Invisible theatre is theatre which has been taken to the streets. The public see events unfolding which they deem to be "real events" but which are later revealed as theatre.

In 1997, Boal was awarded the Career Achievement Award by the Association for Theatre in Higher Education.
