119. SCENE . Inverness. A Room in Macbeth's Castle.

120. LADY MACBETH.

121. Glamis thou art, and Cawdor; and shalt be

122. What thou art promis'd; yet do I fear thy nature;

123. It is too full o' the milk of human kindness

124. To catch the nearest way: " Hie thee hither,

125. That I may pour my spirits in thine ear.

126. The raven himself is hoarse

127. That croaks the fatal entrance of Duncan

128. Under my battlements. Come, you spirits

129. That tend on mortal thoughts, unsex me here;

130. And fill me, from the crown to the toe, top-full

131. Of direst cruelty! Come to my woman's breasts,

132. And take my milk for gall, you murdering ministers,

133. Wherever in your sightless substances

134. You wait on nature's mischief! Come, thick night,

135. And pall thee in the dunnest smoke of hell

136. That my keen knife see not the wound it makes

137. Nor heaven peep through the blanket of the dark

138. To cry, "Hold, hold!"

139. [Enter Macbeth.]

140. Great Glamis! Worthy Cawdor!

141. Greater than both, by the all-hail hereafter!

142. Thy letters have transported me beyond

143. This ignorant present, and I feel now

144. The future in the instant.

145. MACBETH.

146. My dearest love,

147. Duncan comes here tonight.

148. LADY MACBETH.

149. And when goes hence?

150. MACBETH.

151. To-morrow,--as he purposes.

152. LADY MACBETH.

153. O, never

154. Shall sun that morrow see!

155. Your face, my thane, is as a book where men

156. May read strange matters:--to beguile the time,

157. Look like the time; bear welcome in your eye,

158. Your hand, your tongue: look like the innocent flower,

159. But be the serpent under't. He that's coming

160. Must be provided for: and you shall put

161. This night's great business into my despatch.

162. MACBETH.

163. We will speak further.

164. LADY MACBETH.

165. Only look up clear;

166. To alter favor ever is to fear:

167. Leave all the rest to me.

168. . . . .

169. BANQUO.

170. This guest of summer,

171. The temple-haunting martlet, does approve

172. By his lov'd mansionry, that the heaven's breath

173. Smells wooingly here: no jutty, frieze, buttress,

174. Nor coigne of vantage, but this bird hath made

175. His pendant bed and procreant cradle:

176. Where they most breed and haunt, I have observ'd

177. The air is delicate.

178. [Enter Macbeth.]

179. MACBETH.

180. If it were done when 'tis done, then 'twere well

181. It were done quickly. If the assassination

182. Could trammel up the consequence, and catch,

183. With his surcease, success; that but this blow

184. Might be the be-all and the end-all--here,

185. But here, upon this bank and shoal of time,--

186. We'd jump the life to come. . . Besides, this Duncan

187. Hath borne his faculties so meek, hath been

188. So clear in his great office, that his virtues

189. Will plead like angels, trumpet-tongued, against

190. The deep damnation of his taking-off:

191. And pity, like a naked new-born babe,

192. Striding the blast, or heaven's cherubin, hors'd

193. Upon the sightless couriers of the air,

194. Shall blow the horrid deed in every eye,

195. That tears shall drown the wind.--I have no spur

196. To prick the sides of my intent, but only

197. Vaulting ambition, which o'erleaps itself,

198. And falls on the other.

199. [Enter Lady Macbeth.]

200. How now! what news?

201. LADY MACBETH.

202. He has almost supp'd: why have you left the chamber?

203. MACBETH.

204. Hath he ask'd for me?

205. LADY MACBETH.

206. Know you not he has?

207. MACBETH.

208. We will proceed no further in this business:

209. He hath honour'd me of late; and I have bought

210. Golden opinions from all sorts of people,

211. Which would be worn now in their newest gloss,

212. Not cast aside so soon.

213. LADY MACBETH.

214. Was the hope drunk

215. Wherein you dress'd yourself? hath it slept since?

216. And wakes it now, to look so green and pale

217. At what it did so freely? From this time

218. Such I account thy love. Art thou afeard

219. To be the same in thine own act and valor

220. As thou art in desire?

221. MACBETH.

222. Pr'ythee, peace!

223. I dare do all that may become a man;

224. Who dares do more is none.

225. LADY MACBETH.

226. What beast was't, then,

227. That made you break this enterprise to me?

228. When you durst do it, then you were a man;

229. . . . . I have given suck, and know

230. How tender 'tis to love the babe that milks me:

231. I would, while it was smiling in my face,

232. Have pluck'd my nipple from his boneless gums

233. And dash'd the brains out, had I so sworn as you

234. Have done to this.

235. MACBETH.

236. If we should fail?

237. LADY MACBETH.

238. We fail!

239. But screw your courage to the sticking-place,

240. And we'll not fail.

241. MACBETH.

242. I am settled, and bend up

243. Each corporal agent to this terrible feat.

244. Away, and mock the time with fairest show:

245. False face must hide what the false heart doth know.

246. [Exeunt.]

247. BANQUO.

248. A heavy summons lies like lead upon me,

249. And yet I would not sleep:--merciful powers,

250. Restrain in me the cursed thoughts that nature

251. Gives way to in repose!--

252. Who's there?

253. MACBETH.

254. A friend.

255. BANQUO.

256. All's well.

257. I dreamt last night of the three weird sisters:

258. To you they have show'd some truth.

259. MACBETH.

260. I think not of them:

261. Yet, when we can entreat an hour to serve,

262. We would spend it in some words upon that business,

263. If you would grant the time.

264. BANQUO.

265. At your kind'st leisure.

266. MACBETH.

267. If you shall cleave to my consent,--when 'tis,

268. It shall make honor for you.

269. BANQUO.

270. So I lose none

271. In seeking to augment it, but still keep

272. My bosom franchis'd, and allegiance clear,

273. I shall be counsell'd.

274. MACBETH.

275. Good repose the while!

276. BANQUO.

277. Thanks, sir: the like to you!

278. MACBETH

279. Is this a dagger which I see before me,

280. The handle toward my hand? Come, let me clutch thee:--

281. I have thee not, and yet I see thee still.

282. Art thou not, fatal vision, sensible

283. To feeling as to sight? or art thou but

284. A dagger of the mind, a false creation,

285. Proceeding from the heat-oppressed brain?

286. Mine eyes are made the fools o' the other senses,

287. Or else worth all the rest: I see thee still;

288. And on thy blade and dudgeon gouts of blood,

289. Which was not so before.--There's no such thing:

290. It is the bloody business which informs

291. Thus to mine eyes.--Now o'er the one half-world

292. Nature seems dead, and wicked dreams abuse

293. The curtain'd sleep; [. . . ] Thou sure and firm-set earth,

294. Hear not my steps, which way they walk, for fear

295. Thy very stones prate of my whereabout,

296. And take the present horror from the time,

297. Which now suits with it.--Whiles I threat, he lives;

298. Words to the heat of deeds too cold breath gives.

299. [A bell rings.]

300. I go, and it is done; the bell invites me.

301. Hear it not, Duncan, for it is a knell

302. That summons thee to heaven or to hell.

303. [Exit.]

304. [Enter Lady Macbeth.]

305. LADY MACBETH.

306. That which hath made them drunk hath made me bold:

307. What hath quench'd them hath given me fire.--Hark!--Peace!

308. It was the owl that shriek'd, the fatal bellman,

309. Which gives the stern'st good night. He is about it:

310. The doors are open--

311. MACBETH.

312. [Within.] Who's there?--what, ho!

313. [Re-enter Macbeth.]

314. MACBETH.

315. I have done the deed.--Didst thou not hear a noise?

316. LADY MACBETH.

317. I heard the owl scream and the crickets cry.

318. Did not you speak?

319. MACBETH.

320. When?

321. LADY MACBETH.

322. Now.

323. MACBETH.

324. As I descended?

325. LADY MACBETH.

326. Ay.

327. MACBETH. [Looking on his hands.]

328. This is a sorry sight.

329. LADY MACBETH.

330. A foolish thought, to say a sorry sight.

331. These deeds must not be thought

332. After these ways; so, it will make us mad.

333. MACBETH.

334. I heard a voice cry, "Sleep no more!

335. Macbeth does murder sleep,"

336. LADY MACBETH.

337. Who was it that thus cried? Why, worthy thane,

338. You do unbend your noble strength to think

339. So brainsickly of things.--Go get some water,

340. And wash this filthy witness from your hand.--

341. MACBETH.

342. I'll go no more:

343. I am afraid to think what I have done;

344. Look on't again I dare not.

345. LADY MACBETH.

346. Infirm of purpose! The sleeping and the dead

347. Are but as pictures: 'tis the eye of childhood

348. That fears a painted devil.

349. [Exit. Knocking within.]

350. MACBETH.

351. Whence is that knocking?

352. How is't with me, when every noise appals me?

353. What hands are here? Ha, they pluck out mine eyes!

354. Will all great Neptune's ocean wash this blood

355. Clean from my hand? No; this my hand will rather

356. The multitudinous seas incarnadine,

357. Making the green one red.

358. LADY MACBETH.

359. A little water clears us of this deed:

360. How easy is it then! [ . . . ] be not lost

361. So poorly in your thoughts.

362. MACBETH.

363. To know my deed, 'twere best not know myself. [Knocking within.]

364. Wake Duncan with thy knocking! I would thou couldst!

365. [Exeunt.]
