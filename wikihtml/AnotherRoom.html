366. SCENE. Another Room in the Palace.

367. [Enter Lady Macbeth]

368. LADY MACBETH.

369. Naught's had, all's spent,

370. Where our desire is got without content:

371. 'Tis safer to be that which we destroy,

372. Than, by destruction, dwell in doubtful joy.

373. MACBETH.

374. We have scotch'd the snake, not kill'd it;

375. She'll close, and be herself; whilst our poor malice

376. Remains in danger of her former tooth.

377. But let the frame of things disjoint,

378. Both the worlds suffer,

379. Ere we will eat our meal in fear, and sleep

380. In the affliction of these terrible dreams

381. That shake us nightly: better be with the dead,

382. Whom we, to gain our peace, have sent to peace,

383. Than on the torture of the mind to lie

384. In restless ecstasy. Duncan is in his grave;

385. After life's fitful fever he sleeps well;

386. LADY MACBETH.

387. Come on;

388. Gently my lord, sleek o'er your rugged looks;

389. Be bright and jovial 'mong your guests to-night.

390. MACBETH.

391. So shall I, love; and so, I pray, be you:

392. Let your remembrance apply to Banquo;

393. Present him eminence, both with eye and tongue:

394. Unsafe the while, that we

395. Must lave our honors in these flattering streams;

396. And make our faces vizards to our hearts,

397. Disguising what they are.

398. LADY MACBETH.

399. You must leave this.

400. MACBETH.

401. O, full of scorpions is my mind, dear wife!

402. LADY MACBETH.

403. What's to be done?

404. MACBETH.

405. Be innocent of the knowledge, dearest chuck,

406. Till thou applaud the deed. Come, seeling night,

407. Scarf up the tender eye of pitiful day;

408. And with thy bloody and invisible hand

409. Cancel and tear to pieces that great bond

410. Which keeps me pale!--Light thickens; and the crow

411. Makes wing to the rooky wood:

412. Good things of day begin to droop and drowse;

413. Whiles night's black agents to their preys do rouse.--

414. Thou marvell'st at my words: but hold thee still;

415. Things bad begun make strong themselves by ill:

416. [Exeunt.]

417. MACBETH.

418. It will have blood; they say, blood will have blood:

419. Stones have been known to move, and trees to speak;

420. Augurs, and understood relations, have

421. By magot-pies, and choughs, and rooks, brought forth

422. The secret'st man of blood.--What is the night?

423. LADY MACBETH.

424. Almost at odds with morning, which is which.

425. MACBETH.

426. All causes shall give way: I am in blood

427. Step't in so far that, should I wade no more,

428. Returning were as tedious as go o'er:

429. Strange things I have in head, that will to hand;

430. Which must be acted ere they may be scann'd.

431. LADY MACBETH.

432. You lack the season of all natures, sleep.

433. MACBETH.

434. Come, we'll to sleep. My strange and self-abuse

435. Is the initiate fear that wants hard use:--

436. We are yet but young in deed.

437. [Exeunt.]
