Noteson Relativity

A breakdown by chapter:

I. PhysicalMeaningofGeometricalPropositions - Why do we believe what we believe about geometry? We define, point, line, plane and then create axioms from these definitions, these axioms seem true based on the definitions. Any theorem can then be referred to as true if it can be shown to follow from the axioms. Our concept of "truth" in geometry is challenged by euclids parallel postulate which is neither necessary nor provable in geometry. We then assume that the concept of distance holds for points on a rigid body reguardless of the bodies position.

II. TheSystemofCoordinates - Distance on a rigid body is defined in terms of a unit length and geometry. Similarly distance to a point near a rigid body is defined. The cartesian coordinate system (x,y,z) is used. "Every description of events in space involves the use of a rigid body to which such events have to be referred."
