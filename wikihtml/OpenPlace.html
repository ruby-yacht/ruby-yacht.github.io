1. MACBETH

2. Persons Represented

3. MACBETH

4. BANQUO

5. LADY MACBETH

6. THREE WITCHES

7. SCENE I. An open Place. Thunder and Lightning.

8. [Enter three Witches.]

9. FIRST WITCH.

10. When shall we three meet again?

11. In thunder, lightning, or in rain?

12. SECOND WITCH.

13. When the hurlyburly's done,

14. When the battle's lost and won.

15. THIRD WITCH.

16. That will be ere the set of sun.

17. FIRST WITCH.

18. Where the place?

19. SECOND WITCH.

20. Upon the heath.

21. THIRD WITCH.

22. There to meet with Macbeth.

23. ALL.

24. Fair is foul, and foul is fair:

25. Hover through the fog and filthy air.

26. [Witches vanish.]

27. BANQUO.

28. What bloody man is that? He can report,

29. As seemeth by his plight, of the revolt

30. The newest state.[ . . .] pronounce his present death,

31. And with his former title greet Macbeth.

32. What he hath lost, noble Macbeth hath won.

33. [Thunder. Enter the three Witches.]

34. [Drum within.]

35. THIRD WITCH.

36. A drum, a drum!

37. Macbeth doth come.

38. ALL.

39. The weird sisters, hand in hand,

40. Posters of the sea and land,

41. Thus do go about, about:

42. Thrice to thine, and thrice to mine,

43. And thrice again, to make up nine:--

44. Peace!--the charm's wound up.

45. [Enter Macbeth and Banquo.]

46. MACBETH.

47. So foul and fair a day I have not seen.

48. BANQUO.

49. How far is't call'd to Forres?--What are these

50. So wither'd, and so wild in their attire,

51. That look not like the inhabitants o' the earth,

52. And yet are on't?--Live you? or are you aught

53. That man may question?

54. MACBETH.

55. Speak, if you can;--what are you?

56. FIRST WITCH.

57. All hail, Macbeth! hail to thee, Thane of Glamis!

58. SECOND WITCH.

59. All hail, Macbeth! hail to thee, Thane of Cawdor!

60. THIRD WITCH.

61. All hail, Macbeth! that shalt be king hereafter!

62. BANQUO.

63. Good sir, why do you start; and seem to fear

64. Things that do sound so fair?-- I' the name of truth,

65. Are ye fantastical? [ . . . ] My noble partner

66. You greet with present grace and great prediction

67. Of noble having and of royal hope. . . .

68. If you can look into the seeds of time,

69. And say which grain will grow, and which will not,

70. Speak then to me, who neither beg nor fear

71. Your favors nor your hate.

72. FIRST WITCH.

73. Hail!

74. SECOND WITCH.

75. Hail!

76. THIRD WITCH.

77. Hail!

78. FIRST WITCH.

79. Lesser than Macbeth, and greater.

80. SECOND WITCH.

81. Not so happy, yet much happier.

82. THIRD WITCH.

83. Thou shalt get kings, though thou be none:

84. So all hail, Macbeth and Banquo!

85. FIRST WITCH.

86. Banquo and Macbeth, all hail!

87. MACBETH.

88. Stay, you imperfect speakers. Say from whence

89. You owe this strange intelligence?

90. [Witches vanish.]

91. BANQUO.

92. The earth hath bubbles, as the water has,

93. And these are of them:--whither are they vanish'd?

94. MACBETH.

95. Your children shall be kings.

96. BANQUO.

97. You shall be king.

98. MACBETH

99. Do you not hope your children shall be kings?

100. BANQUO.

101. 'Tis strange: oftentimes to win us to our harm,

102. The instruments of darkness tell us truths;

103. Win us with honest trifles, to betray's

104. In deepest consequence.--

105. MACBETH.

106. [Aside.] This supernatural soliciting

107. Cannot be ill; cannot be good:--if ill,

108. Why hath it given me earnest of success,

109. Commencing in a truth? I am Thane of Cawdor:

110. If good, why do I yield to that suggestion

111. Whose horrid image doth unfix my hair,

112. And make my seated heart knock at my ribs,

113. Against the use of nature? Present fears

114. Are less than horrible imaginings:

115. My thought, whose murder yet is but fantastical,

116. Shakes so my single state of mind, that function

117. Is smother'd in surmise; and nothing is

118. But what is not.

(macbeth heads in and down from building)
