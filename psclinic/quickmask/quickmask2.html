An introduction to the Quick-Mask

In this lesson, we want to cut out an element from a photo so that we can put it into another photo. to do this, we are using the Quick Mask mode.

Files for the lesson are available at this link: Quick Mask Lesson Files (Save this file)

  1. In the bottom left of the toolbar, you will see the quick mask icon. Click this to enter Quick Mask mode.
  2. In Quick Mask mode, use your paint tools, such as the pencil or brush, to "paint" a selection in. Use black and white.
  3. Start tracing the edge of the object using black and the brush tool. You will see red instead of black if you are painting in Quick Mask mode. Red will be your selection when you exit the Quick Mask mode.
  4. If you make a mistake, paint over the mistake with white. Hit X to switch your colors.
  5. When you have your object outlined, and you have checked all of the details to make sure that you have them all, select the paint bucket tool (G on the keyboard) and fill in the object.
  6. If you had used a soft brush, make sure that the paint bucket adequetly filled in the object. You may need to go back in with the brush if necessary.
  7. Exit the Quick Mask mode and see what your selection looks like. When you are satisfied with your results, you will need to save the selection.
  8. To save your selection, go to the Select drop-down menu on the top of the screen, and choose "Save Selection". Name your selection something you will remember, and hit "OK".
  9. Whenever you want to bring up that selection, go back to the Select menu and choose "load selection". Find your saved selection in the "Channel" menu, and hit OK.
  10. You have successfully used the Quick-Mask to create a selection!
