An introduction to the Blending elements into a photograph

You will need the Armorman and Cars PSD files here to complete this exercise.

  1. Open armorman.psd, load the Armorman selection, copy and paste into cars.psd.
  2. With the layer that the Armorman is on as the active layer, hit Command-T on the keyboard to apply a Free Transform on the Armorman.
  3. The Armorman will now be surrounded by a box with handles. Click and hold outside the box to rotate the Armorman, click and hold the handles to resize, and click and hold inside the box to move.
  4. Position the Armorman in the sky, but partially covered by the middle-ground.
  5. Load the Sky selection and click on the Background Layer.
  6. Invert the selection (Select menu, Inverse) and copy the Background.
  7. Paste into a new layer and drag the Armorman layer under the new layer, but above the background.
  8. Copy and paste a new Armorman into cars.psd and position it in the middle ground.
  9. Load the Car and Pole selection.
  10. Invert the selection, and with the new Armorman layer active, create a Layer Mask.
  11. Select the background Armorman by holding the Command button on the keyboard (Control on the PC), and clicking on the icon of the layer on the Layers tab.
  12. Create an Brightness/Contrast adjustment layer by clicking the Adjustment Layer button at the bottom of the Layers tab.
  13. Adjust the Brightness and Contrast of the background Armorman so that it blends better with the background.

You have successfully blended in pasted elements from one photo into another! Practice makes perfect, try it again and see how good you can get!
